2. Scope & offline-first design

IdealPartner is offline-first. When you are not signed in, the App runs locally on your device and your data does not leave the device. All coaching content — lessons, drills, and scenario “games” — is hand-authored and processed locally. There is no runtime artificial-intelligence or large-language-model processing of your data.

When you create an account and sign in, certain data is synced to our backend so it is available across your sessions and devices and so that social features work. This policy covers both modes and explains which data is which.

10. Social & community features

IdealPartner includes optional social features. Please share thoughtfully — content you post may be visible to others as described:

  • Friends are added only by private friend code. There is no public user directory.
  • Leagues / cohorts — within your weekly cohort, your display name and streak are visible to other members of that cohort.
  • Co-op teams of 2–5 people can exchange team chat messages visible to team members.
  • Community feed — a global, public feed of text/photo/poll posts, likes, and poll votes. Content posted here is visible to other users of the App. The feed includes reporting, moderation, and user blocking.
  • Coin gifting — you can gift in-app coins to friends.

Your email address is never shown publicly to other users. Avoid posting sensitive personal details in public areas.

17. Security

We use technical and organizational measures appropriate to the risk, including:

  • Encryption in transit (HTTPS/TLS) and encryption at rest via our hosting provider.
  • Authentication tokens stored in the iOS Keychain; passwords handled through our authentication provider using industry-standard hashing.
  • Access controls and the principle of least privilege for our systems.
  • On-device downscaling/compression of photos before upload.
  • Database access controls provided by our hosting platform, regular platform and dependency updates, and administrative access limited to the developer.

No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.
